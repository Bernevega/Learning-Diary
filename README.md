# C# LEARNING DIARY

## About this diary
This diary is a record of my C# learning journey.
I am studying C# day by day with the goal of reaching a very high level of understanding, from the absolute basics to advanced and professional-level topics.

This diary helps me keep track of:
- what I studied;
- what tasks I completed;
- what I understood well;
- what I still need to practice;
- how much progress I have made overall.

## How I am learning
I am learning C# in a structured day-by-day way.
Each day has its own topic or theme.
For example, one day may focus on variables, another on loops, another on methods, classes, arrays, properties, and so on.

The goal is not to rush through topics, but to study each one properly before moving on.
I want to understand not only how to write the code, but also:
- why it works;
- when it should be used;
- what mistakes can happen;
- how it connects to other parts of C#.

## How I use ChatGPT
I use ChatGPT as a learning partner and study assistant during this journey.

ChatGPT helps me by:
- explaining C# topics in simple language;
- creating daily theory and practice tasks;
- adjusting the difficulty to my current level;
- checking my code and pointing out mistakes;
- helping me understand why something is wrong;
- helping me write summaries of what I learned;
- helping me track my long-term progress.

I do not use ChatGPT just to get answers.
I use it as a tool to help me actually learn, practice, and understand C# step by step.

## How each diary day works
Usually, each learning day includes:
1. a specific C# topic or theme;
2. explanation/theory about that topic;
3. practical coding tasks;
4. me writing the code myself;
5. checking what I did correctly and what needs improvement;
6. a short diary summary of what I learned that day.

This means the diary is not only a list of notes.
It is also proof of active practice.

## What is included in the diary
My diary entries usually contain:
- the day number;
- the topic of that day;
- task descriptions;
- what I practiced;
- what I learned;
- a conclusion for the day;
- my overall progress percentage.

This makes it easy to look back and see how my skills are improving over time.

## My learning style
I prefer learning in a very detailed and structured way.
I do not want to just memorize syntax.
I want to build a strong foundation and understand C# deeply.

Because of that, my learning process focuses on:
- mastering basics before moving forward;
- practicing with many small tasks;
- revisiting weak areas when needed;
- learning topics in logical order;
- building confidence step by step.
